# Sora Single Prompt - 2026-03-25 Reel

## Purpose

Use this when the input is only a short Mitozz bottle video clip and Sora needs to generate the rest of the reel around it.

This is the single-prompt version of the March 25 reel direction.

## Recommended Setup

- `format`: `Instagram Reel`
- `aspect_ratio`: `9:16`
- `runtime`: `10 to 12 seconds`
- `input_mode`: `video-to-video` or `video-conditioned generation`
- `source_clip_role`: `bottle identity and movement anchor`

## Single Prompt

```text
Create a premium Japanese wellness Instagram reel in true 9:16 vertical format, around 11 seconds long, using the provided Mitozz bottle clip as the core product anchor. The final reel should feel like one calm editorial morning sequence, not a supplement ad.

Story direction:
Start with a quiet early-morning apartment workspace in Tokyo with soft steel-blue daylight coming from a window. Show a Japanese woman in her early 30s, calm, polished, and slightly reflective, seated at a clean wooden desk in neutral soft clothing. The mood is not stressed or dramatic; it is a subtle pause before a busy day begins. Then move into a closer lifestyle detail of the same desk world: hand, mug, notebook, curtain light, and soft negative space. Then let the provided Mitozz bottle clip become the natural reveal inside that same environment, keeping the exact real bottle identity from the input clip. End on a calmer, cleaner held frame where the bottle remains steady in the same workspace with more breathing room for end text.

Creative intent:
This reel is about rethinking daily condition from “push harder” to “support more intentionally.” The emotional progression should be: pause, reframe, reveal, settle. The reel should feel premium, quiet, intelligent, and emotionally restrained.

Visual world:
Quiet premium apartment workspace, wood desk, cloud-white and steel-blue morning atmosphere, soft daylight, minimal props, neutral wardrobe, airy negative space, editorial calm. Keep the same woman, same room family, same desk, same lighting direction, and same mood across the whole reel.

Bottle rules:
Use the provided bottle clip as the exact truth for bottle identity, label structure, silhouette, cap finish, and natural movement. Do not redesign, relabel, recolor, enlarge, stylize, or beautify the bottle into an ad object. Keep the bottle at a believable one-hand supplement-bottle scale in the environment. The bottle should feel modest and realistic relative to the hand and tabletop, not oversized.

Camera and motion:
Use restrained editorial motion only. The opening can have a very subtle push-in. The reframe can have a gentle lateral drift. The bottle reveal should feel like a soft continuation, not a dramatic hero shot. The end should almost hold still. All transitions should feel clean and natural, not flashy.

Text and layout:
Do not bake in any text, subtitles, logos, stickers, UI, or CTA graphics. Preserve generous text-safe space, especially near the top and lower third, so text can be added later in editing.

What to avoid:
No loud supplement-ad energy, no hard-sell beauty-commercial look, no cluttered desk, no brand logos on laptop or objects, no flashy camera moves, no dramatic acting, no medical CGI look, no warped hands, no extra props, no extra people, no bottle identity drift, no oversized bottle, no fake productivity-hack aesthetic.

Target feeling:
A calm morning reset reel that quietly introduces Mitozz as part of a more intentional daily rhythm.
```

## Negative Prompt

```text
oversized bottle, bottle redesign, wrong label, silver cap, warped hands, extra fingers, extra people, visible brand logos, text on objects, baked-in captions, fake UI, cluttered workspace, harsh ad lighting, beauty-commercial styling, medical CGI, loud supplement ad energy, flashy transitions, unrelated room changes, identity drift
```

## Editing Intent After Generation

If the generated reel is successful, add text in edit only:

1. `頑張る前に、整える。`
2. `刺激より、毎日の設計へ。`
3. `整えるという選択。`
4. `仕組みをやさしく読む`

Keep the text minimal, calm, and premium.
