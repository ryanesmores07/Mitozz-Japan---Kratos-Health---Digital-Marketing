## Creative Objective

Translate the phrase "supporting mitochondria" into everyday, non-technical understanding so it feels clear, trustworthy, and usable rather than vague or overclaimed.

## Target Persona

`Research-First Wellness Optimizer`

They want logic, structure, and careful language. They dislike empty wellness phrases and respond better when complex ideas are translated into clean daily meaning.

## Format Decision

`carousel`

Why:

- this topic is a definition problem, so it benefits from a calm sequence
- the viewer needs concept translation more than product promotion
- five slides are enough to move from phrase clarification to practical meaning without making it dense

- `template_set`: `Set 1 + Set 2`
- `slide_blueprint`: `5-slide`

## Creative Direction

- `core_concept`: supporting mitochondria means supporting the conditions they depend on, not chasing one magic switch
- `angle`: replace vague science language with calm daily translation
- `immediate_takeaway`: the viewer should understand that "support" means sleep, nutrition, movement, and steady daily conditions working together
- `viewer_feeling`: clearer, less intimidated, more trusting
- `positioning`: smart, medically responsible, premium, non-dramatic
- `what_to_avoid`:
  - cell-visual cliches
  - loud science graphics
  - empty buzzword phrasing
  - overpromising copy
  - dense textbook-style explanation

## Layout / Structure

5-slide carousel:

1. Cover
   - Set H pacing
   - one strong definition-led hook
   - lower structure band with three restrained semantic cues over a fresh Nano Banana editorial source plate
   - use translated daily-life wording on that band, not the literal `睡眠 / 食事 / 運動` trio, so the cover frames the idea instead of duplicating the explanatory slides

2. Phrase clarification
   - Set C answer-card logic
   - explain what "supporting mitochondria" does not mean
   - one compact card, one short translation line

3. Daily translation
   - Set D logic framing
   - show that support comes from conditions, not a single isolated action
   - three-column support model with icons and short labels

4. Practical reading lens
   - editorial explainer card
   - teach what to look at in daily life when thinking about support
   - concise cues only, not a checklist overload

5. Save close
   - calm summary card
   - reinforce the softer definition
   - close with a daily-conditions summary rather than restating the exact trio again
   - save-oriented ending without social clutter
