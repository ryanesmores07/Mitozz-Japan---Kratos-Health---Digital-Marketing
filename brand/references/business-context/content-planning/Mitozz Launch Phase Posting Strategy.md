# Mitozz Launch Phase Posting Strategy

## Purpose

Use this strategy when the account is still at or near zero followers.

At this stage, the account should optimize for discovery and first trust, not for heavy Story volume.

## Core Principle

Feed and Reels create reach.

Stories support that reach.

Do not expect Stories to carry growth before the account has an audience base.

## Launch Phase Cadence

Default weekly cadence:

- `3` feed posts per week
- at least `1` Reel per week
- Stories on feed or Reel days by default
- maximum `1` extra standalone Story day per week

This keeps output intentional and efficient.

## Priority Order

When capacity is limited, prioritize in this order:

1. Reel
2. save-worthy education carousel
3. same-day support Story
4. one extra interactive or proof Story
5. optional standalone Story beyond that

## Story Rule In Launch Phase

Stories should mainly do these jobs:

- route to the latest feed post
- make the account feel active
- support Highlights
- occasionally gather lightweight signals

Do not judge Stories by poll votes yet.

Early Story success should be measured more by:

- account activity
- profile credibility
- highlight building
- occasional replies or taps

## Reuse Rule

In launch phase, reuse is good when it happens through:

- repeated story templates
- evergreen topics
- Highlights
- refreshed copy on proven structures

Do not overproduce brand-new story systems every week.

## March Application Rule

For the current March batch:

- keep the existing feed plan
- keep same-day support Stories
- keep one extra interactive Story for signal-gathering
- treat later standalone Stories as optional when capacity is tight

## Decision Rule

If a Story does not clearly support one of these:

- a new feed post
- a new Reel
- an interactive signal
- a trust/highlight asset

then it should be considered optional during launch phase.
