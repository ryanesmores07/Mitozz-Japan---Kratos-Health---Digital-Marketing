# Mitozz Locked Bottle Workflow

## Purpose

Use this workflow whenever the Mitozz bottle is a visible subject and pack fidelity matters.

This workflow exists because fully AI-regenerated bottle renders may drift from the real package design.

The rule is simple:

- the bottle stays real
- the scene adapts around it
- the final image must still feel naturally lit, realistic, and premium

## When To Use

Use the locked bottle workflow for:

- `Set 3` product hero posts
- product-led carousel end cards
- product relevance frames
- reel end frames with bottle visibility
- any asset where the bottle label needs to stay truthful

## Production Rule

Do not ask the generator to redesign or repaint the bottle.

Instead:

1. use the real bottle image as the visible product source
2. design the environment, background, lighting mood, and negative space around it
3. if AI editing is used, preserve the exact bottle identity and only harmonize the scene around it

## Realism Checklist

The bottle must feel visually native to the scene.

Check these every time:

- light direction on the bottle matches light direction in the environment
- shadow softness matches the rest of the frame
- reflection intensity matches the surface material
- bottle scale feels realistic relative to hands, cups, books, or tabletop objects
- use [Mitozz Bottle Size Spec.md](/Users/ernieryanesmores/Desktop/Workspace/Mitozz-Japan---Kratos-Health---Digital-Marketing/brand/references/business-context/visual/Mitozz%20Bottle%20Size%20Spec.md) whenever the bottle appears near a hand or in a lifestyle desk scene
- bottle contact with the surface feels grounded, not floating
- bottle height feels believable for the desk scene and should usually sit around mug-height or slightly taller, not dominate the tabletop
- color temperature of the bottle highlights matches the scene
- edge sharpness does not make the bottle look pasted in
- background blur level feels consistent with bottle focus

## Lighting Rules

Preferred lighting:

- soft daylight
- diffused studio daylight
- cloud-white base
- mist-blue atmosphere
- restrained apricot warmth as a secondary glow

Avoid:

- hard spotlighting that does not match the original bottle image
- glossy reflections that make the pack look synthetic
- dark ad-style contrast
- neon highlights

## Scene Matching Rules

When placing the bottle into a premium environment:

- keep the bottle on a believable surface
- match horizon and camera height
- keep perspective natural
- avoid oversized bottle scale
- if a hand is present, the bottle should follow the locked size ranges in [Mitozz Bottle Size Spec.md](/Users/ernieryanesmores/Desktop/Workspace/Mitozz-Japan---Kratos-Health---Digital-Marketing/brand/references/business-context/visual/Mitozz%20Bottle%20Size%20Spec.md), not enlarged into a hero object unless the frame is a pure product hero
- in lifestyle scenes, keep the bottle modest in scale and secondary to the overall environment unless it is a pure product hero
- use only a small number of props
- keep the scene quieter than the product

Good supports:

- clean tabletop
- folded cloth
- soft notebook or book
- cup or glass
- minimal tray

Avoid:

- cluttered supplement scenes
- too many pills or capsules
- noisy skincare prop piles
- dramatic luxury staging

## Set 3 Specific Rule

For `Set 3`, default to:

- real bottle kept intact
- editorial environment generated or composed around it
- large negative space
- restrained headline
- premium cool-to-warm atmosphere

If the bottle cannot stay truthful in a generated output, reject the image and use the real bottle workflow instead of forcing a full AI render.

## Set 4 Specific Rule

For `Set 4`, only include the bottle if:

- it remains secondary in the scene
- scale feels natural
- the scene is realistic enough to hold it

If the bottle starts to feel fake or distracting, remove it and let lifestyle carry the frame.

## Prompting Rule

When building prompts for bottle-led assets, explicitly state:

- `keep the real bottle design intact`
- `do not redesign, recolor, simplify, or relabel the bottle`
- `match surrounding light direction and shadow behavior to the bottle`
- `make the bottle feel grounded on the surface`
- `preserve realistic scale and contact shadow`
- `make the bottle slightly smaller if it starts to feel oversized in the environment`
- `adapt bottle highlights and shadow softness to the surrounding desk light`
- `use the locked Mitozz bottle size spec for believable bottle scale`

## Approval Standard

Approve a bottle-led asset only if:

- the bottle still looks like the real Mitozz package
- the environment feels premium and natural
- the bottle does not look pasted in
- lighting feels consistent across the full frame
- the image still belongs to the Steel Light system
