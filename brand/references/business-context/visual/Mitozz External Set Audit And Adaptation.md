# Mitozz External Set Audit And Adaptation

## Purpose

This document audits the newly added external reference sets under:

- `brand/references/business-context/visual/reference-pack/style-anchors/Set A`
- `brand/references/business-context/visual/reference-pack/style-anchors/Set B`
- `brand/references/business-context/visual/reference-pack/style-anchors/Set C`
- `brand/references/business-context/visual/reference-pack/style-anchors/Set D`
- `brand/references/business-context/visual/reference-pack/style-anchors/Set E`
- `brand/references/business-context/visual/reference-pack/style-anchors/Set F`
- `brand/references/business-context/visual/reference-pack/style-anchors/Set G`
- `brand/references/business-context/visual/reference-pack/style-anchors/Set H`

The goal is not to copy those brands literally.
The goal is to extract the reusable structural logic, reject the low-fit visual habits, and translate the strongest patterns into the Mitozz system.

## Strategic Call

These references are useful because they show repeatable social-design behavior, not because they are already premium enough for Mitozz.

The strongest takeaway is:

- they are clearly built from reusable master layouts
- they reuse cover, detail, comparison, and CTA modules repeatedly
- they color-code variants aggressively
- they rely on isolated product packshots, flat-lay plates, and repeated text blocks
- they use the same closing CTA or follow slide often

That means the real value is in:

- sequence logic
- modular slide roles
- category framing
- comparison structures

The weak point is usually:

- mass-market typography
- crowded information density
- campaign badges and giveaway language
- generic social CTA endings

Mitozz should borrow the structure and upgrade the art direction.

## How These Sets Were Likely Built

This is an inference from the visual evidence.

Most of these sets appear to be built from one duplicated master per campaign or product family:

- one cover master
- one or two body-slide masters
- one detail-card master
- one closing CTA master

Common production behaviors:

- isolated product cutouts placed on fixed backgrounds
- flat-lay or tabletop photos reused across multiple slides
- one accent color per product type or concern
- text blocks swapped without changing the base geometry
- same arrows, rays, speech bubbles, and highlight bands reused repeatedly
- final follow or campaign slides templated almost exactly

Operational lesson for Mitozz:

- build page-role modules, not one-off full-post designs
- lock the geometry first
- let AI change plates, not hierarchy

## What Mitozz Should Borrow Across The Whole Pack

- selector logic
- decision-tree logic
- FAQ sequencing
- close-up product staging
- problem -> explanation -> practical translation pacing
- color-coded sub-logic when it helps categorization

## What Mitozz Should Reject Across The Whole Pack

- giveaway octagons and promo seals as a default behavior
- follow-and-like mechanics baked into the artwork
- cute sparkles, doodle rays, and decorative clutter
- too many boxed claims on one slide
- busy product-catalog feeling
- striped or patterned backgrounds used without restraint
- generic social-media footer slides

## Set-By-Set Audit

### Set A

Observed pattern:

- product-chooser carousel
- mono-color campaign cover with several SKUs
- multi-card selector grid
- numbered detail continuation slides
- repeated follow or account CTA ending

Used for:

- helping the audience choose between product forms
- product-fit framing
- light campaign or seasonal selection logic

Strong borrowable pattern:

- the selector grid is very reusable
- the slide role sequence is clean: hook -> choose -> detail -> close

What to reject:

- sweet pink palette
- soft bokeh dots
- retail-heavy feeling
- overly obvious "check your type" sales framing

Mitozz adaptation:

- use the selector structure for persona-fit or routine-fit education
- turn product choice into knowledge choice
- examples:
  - `どの生活リズムに近い？`
  - `まず見直したい土台はどれ？`
  - `今の自分に近い感覚は？`

Internal mapping:

- use as an `E2` comparison / selector module

Priority:

- `medium`

### Set B

Observed pattern:

- seasonal product lineup cover
- stacked list-detail slides
- color-coded product tags
- category-comparison layout
- standard follow / save ending

Used for:

- item lineup overviews
- SKU-by-SKU comparison
- seasonal recommendations

Strong borrowable pattern:

- product taxonomy made visually scan-friendly

What to reject:

- catalog feeling
- too much list behavior for a one-product brand
- mass-market seasonal retail styling

Mitozz adaptation:

- low direct fit right now because Mitozz is not a broad multi-SKU catalog
- keep only the idea of clean grouped comparison if future product families expand

Internal mapping:

- mostly `P2` if a future lineup exists

Priority:

- `low`

### Set C

Observed pattern:

- Q-and-A cover
- one question per follow-up slide
- strong numbered rhythm on the left rail
- isolated hero product on white
- short answer block plus explanation body

Used for:

- FAQ
- recommendation rounds
- seasonal care questions
- answer-led education

Strong borrowable pattern:

- this is one of the best structural references in the pack
- the numbered answer-card format is reusable and clear

What to reject:

- heavy left-side rail treatment
- pink body copy
- too much catalog-brand specificity

Mitozz adaptation:

- use for:
  - myth vs clarity
  - FAQ
  - "what does this actually mean?" posts
  - objection handling

Best upgrade move:

- replace the left beige rail with a cleaner Mitozz white-card edge treatment
- keep one accent line only
- reduce copy by 15 to 25 percent

Internal mapping:

- `E2` FAQ module
- `T1` reassurance module

Priority:

- `high`

### Set D

Observed pattern:

- decision-tree cover
- A-to-E answer branches
- one detail card per branch
- strong concern-color coding
- striped neutral background and big category letter

Used for:

- self-diagnosis
- recommendation charts
- choose-your-match logic

Strong borrowable pattern:

- this is the strongest branching-system reference in the pack
- the cover chart plus branch detail sequence is extremely reusable

What to reject:

- striped wallpaper background
- oversized alphabetical labeling
- obvious cosmetic catalog feel

Mitozz adaptation:

- use for:
  - `どの生活パターンに近い？`
  - `今見直したいのは何から？`
  - `まず理解したいのは基礎 / 習慣 / 成分 / 相性のどれ？`

Best upgrade move:

- convert the branching chart into cleaner floating nodes inside the Mitozz white-card system
- keep the branch letter or number very small
- use one mist-blue system with restrained accent tint changes instead of many pastel circles

Internal mapping:

- `E2` decision-tree module

Priority:

- `high`

### Set H

Observed pattern:

- supplement education carousel
- problem statement cover
- ingredient explanation slides
- "food equivalent" proof slide
- ease-of-use demonstration
- supporting ingredient panels

Used for:

- category education
- ingredient justification
- supplement logic
- credibility building

Strong borrowable pattern:

- this is the highest-fit supplement-education reference in the pack
- the pacing logic is excellent even though the visuals are not premium enough for Mitozz

What to reject:

- busy infographic density
- loud red warning text
- too many cutout foods and ingredient splashes on one slide
- grey utilitarian look

Mitozz adaptation:

- borrow the pacing, not the finish
- best sequence for Mitozz:
  1. problem or misconception
  2. what the mechanism means
  3. why food-only framing is incomplete or impractical
  4. how Mitozz fits a calm routine
  5. ingredient or formulation support
  6. soft save or read-more CTA

Internal mapping:

- `E1` hook
- `E2` explanation
- `L1` routine translation
- `P1` product relevance close

Priority:

- `high`

## Mitozz Adaptation Rules

When adapting these references, keep:

- the modular sequence
- the slide role logic
- the decision-tree and selector behavior
- the product isolation discipline

When adapting these references, change:

- palette to Steel Light
- typography to the Mitozz premium stack
- spacing to a calmer and more editorial rhythm
- CTA language to save, learn, or reflect rather than retail urgency
- image treatment to softer, cleaner, more restrained lighting

## Typography Upgrade Call

The references prove that structure matters more than fancy typography, but the typography is also the biggest place where Mitozz can look more premium than they do.

Working decision:

- primary Japanese family = `Hiragino Sans`
- editorial accent = `Hiragino Mincho ProN` in very short doses only
- no dense slide should behave like a decorative serif layout

Use the serif accent only for:

- section labels
- one short pull word
- a short number-led headline moment
- premium trust or product covers

Do not use the serif accent for:

- inner educational paragraphs
- long FAQ answers
- dense CTA slides

## Final Recommendation

Use these references as structural input, not aesthetic truth.

Highest-priority borrow list:

1. `Set H` pacing
2. `Set D` decision-tree logic
3. `Set C` FAQ answer cards
4. `Set E` product beauty-shot simplicity
5. `Set A` selector-grid behavior

That combination is strong enough to upgrade the Mitozz system without diluting its premium science-first identity.
