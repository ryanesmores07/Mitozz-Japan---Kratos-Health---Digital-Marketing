# Brand Visual Direction

## Purpose

This document defines a lean visual direction for Instagram so the creative director can plan and execute posts with speed, consistency, and strong organic appeal.

## Core Direction

The visual world should feel:

- airy
- clinical but not cold
- premium but not intimidating
- calm, luminous, and highly touchable
- modern Japanese skincare with editorial restraint

The inspiration points clearly toward a soft steel-blue foundation with warm apricot-orange highlights. That balance feels stronger than a pure steel-blue-only route because it creates both trust and memorability.

## Brand Mood

Working theme name:

Steel Light

Mood sentence:

Soft science, clear skin, quiet confidence.

## Visual Principles

1. Lead with white space.
2. Use cool steel-blue as the atmosphere, not as a heavy block.
3. Use warm orange only as a signal color for energy, ingredients, CTA emphasis, and product contrast.
4. Make product images feel backlit, clean, and almost floating.
5. Keep layouts sparse and intentional. One idea per frame.
6. Favor calm clarity over busy beauty-brand decoration.

## Color System

Primary palette:

- Cloud White: #F7FAFC
- Mist Blue: #DCE8EE
- Steel Blue: #6F8794
- Mineral Navy: #22323B
- Apricot Glow: #F48A5A

Supporting palette:

- Soft Peach Tint: #F7E5DC
- Frost Silver: #EEF2F4
- Warm Sand: #E9DDD3

Usage guidance:

- 60% Cloud White / very pale neutral space
- 20% Mist Blue / cool atmosphere and backgrounds
- 10% Steel Blue / structure, dividers, cool type accents
- 8% Apricot Glow / highlights, CTA cues, ingredient energy
- 2% Mineral Navy / premium depth, small text, key anchors

Do not use saturated royal blue, pure black, or neon orange.

## Typography

Keep the system to two families.

Primary body and Japanese copy:

- Noto Sans JP
- Weights: Regular, Medium, Bold

Editorial accent for English words, campaign titles, and premium moments:

- Cormorant Garamond
- Weights: SemiBold, Italic

Typography rules:

- Japanese educational text should stay in Noto Sans JP for readability.
- Use the serif only for short English accents, not dense paragraphs.
- Headline spacing should feel open and elegant.
- Avoid more than two font families in one asset.
- Keep text blocks short and save-heavy rather than caption-heavy on the artwork.

## Instagram Design System

### Feed Look

The feed should alternate across three visual modes:

1. Cool product light
2. White editorial education
3. Warm human or expert trust

This creates a grid that feels breathable, premium, and varied without losing cohesion.

### Recommended Post Types

- Educational carousels with one strong hook and one idea per slide
- Product hero posts with a luminous cool-to-warm gradient
- Expert or founder credibility posts
- Ingredient explainers
- Routine and how-to posts
- Social proof or testimonial cards with clean portrait framing

### Cover Slide Rules

- One headline only
- Large white or pale background
- One product or one simple supporting visual
- Accent color used sparingly
- The promise should feel save-worthy, not salesy

## Composition Rules

- Use large margins
- Centered or slightly offset compositions work best
- Let the product occupy 25% to 45% of the frame
- Use soft shadows and glow, never harsh drop shadows
- When using people, prefer calm expressions and clean poses
- Backgrounds should feel luminous, frosted, or lightly washed

## Photo and Render Direction

Products:

- frosted light
- subtle reflections
- minimal surfaces
- translucent highlights
- no clutter

People:

- clean skin
- gentle confidence
- natural makeup
- soft daylight or diffused studio light
- quiet luxury styling

Avoid:

- dark moody lighting
- aggressive beauty retouching
- loud props
- busy lifestyle scenes
- trend-driven collage graphics

## Graphic Elements

Use only a few consistent devices:

- soft gradient washes
- thin rules
- rounded highlight tags
- small editorial labels
- restrained underlines or highlight bars

Do not overbuild the system with too many badges, icons, stickers, or decorative shapes.

## Organic Growth Guidance

The design should support saves and shares first.

Priority content mix:

- 70% education
- 20% authority and trust
- 10% conversion

Creative rules for organic growth:

- Start carousels with a problem, payoff, or myth-busting hook
- Keep each slide focused on one idea
- Make slide 2 to slide 5 valuable enough to save
- Use visual consistency so the brand is recognizable even without the logo
- Design for quick mobile reading with short lines and strong hierarchy
- End with a light save/share prompt, not a hard sell

## Content Pillars

1. Skin science made understandable
2. Daily ritual and product use
3. Ingredient clarity
4. Expert validation
5. Transformation through consistency, not hype

## Recommended Grid Rhythm

Use a repeating nine-post rhythm:

1. Educational white card
2. Cool product hero
3. Expert or human portrait
4. Ingredient explainer
5. Product ritual
6. Educational white card
7. Social proof or authority
8. Cool product hero
9. Soft human skin result image

This creates the same balance shown in the references: clean information, luminous product focus, and warm human trust.

## Tone for On-Canvas Copy

Copy should feel:

- intelligent
- calm
- lightly editorial
- never loud
- never pushy

Preferred style:

- short educational hooks
- elegant English campaign accents
- concise Japanese body copy
- clear benefit framing

## Design Do / Do Not

Do:

- use steel-blue atmosphere with warm orange signals
- keep layouts minimal
- let the product glow
- use white space as a luxury device
- make education feel beautiful

Do not:

- fill every area
- use too many font styles
- overuse gradients
- make every post a sales ad
- chase fast-moving design trends that reduce trust

## Final Recommendation

Move forward with a steel-blue-led direction elevated by soft apricot warmth.

This is the strongest match to the inspiration because it gives:

- scientific trust
- premium clarity
- organic-feed distinctiveness
- flexibility for both product and educational content

If the team wants a shorthand:

Luminous clinical minimalism with warm vitamin energy.
