# Mitozz Instagram Story Strategy

## Purpose

This file defines how Mitozz should use Instagram Stories as a growth and trust layer.

Stories should not behave like a smaller version of the feed.
They should behave like the brand's live conversation layer:

- lighter
- more interactive
- more human
- faster to consume
- still premium

## Core Principle

Do not copy other brands' visuals directly.
Copy their operating strategy:

- use Stories to guide traffic
- use Stories to build habit
- use Stories to gather signals
- use Stories to show proof
- use Stories to feel alive between feed posts

For Mitozz, Stories should support the feed, not compete with it.

## What Strong Brands Actually Do

Successful brands usually treat Stories as a mix of:

- feed amplification
- interaction
- proof
- behind-the-scenes texture
- quick education
- CTA routing

They do not rely on one mode only.

The winning pattern is usually:

- polished brand frame
- human or contextual frame
- native interaction or CTA

## Mitozz Story Role

Mitozz Stories should do 4 jobs:

1. reinforce feed posts
2. add a more human or daily-life layer
3. create low-friction interaction
4. move people to the next action

That next action can be:

- view the feed post
- tap a link sticker
- answer a poll
- reply to a question box
- revisit a highlight

## Weekly Story Mix

Use this as the default weekly split:

- `30%` `Set 1` text-led education or feed reinforcement
- `30%` realistic lifestyle or daily-context Stories
- `20%` interactive Stories
- `20%` proof or trust Stories

This keeps the account:

- consistent
- alive
- useful
- less repetitive than a text-only system

## Approved Story Types

### 1. Feed Reinforcement Story

Use when:

- a feed carousel or reel needs another touchpoint

Structure:

- hook
- one takeaway
- CTA back to feed

Best template:

- simplified `Set 1`

### 2. Daily Context Story

Use when:

- we want the brand to feel real and lived-in

Structure:

- routine moment
- short contextual line
- soft perspective or question

Best template:

- `Set 4`

### 3. Interactive Story

Use when:

- we want taps, votes, replies, or audience signals

Formats:

- poll
- slider
- question box
- quiz

Best template:

- `Set 1` or `Set 4`, simplified heavily

### 4. Proof / Trust Story

Use when:

- we want reassurance, authority, or credibility

Formats:

- repost
- founder or expert comment
- trust statement
- customer signal

Best template:

- `Set 5` with `Set 1` clarity

### 5. Story CTA / Route Story

Use when:

- we want to move people somewhere specific

Destinations:

- feed post
- product page
- article or landing page
- highlight

Rule:

- keep the visual elegant
- let the native Instagram sticker do the click work

## Are Images Required?

No.
It is okay not to use images sometimes.

But text-only should not become the whole story strategy.

Use this rule:

- text-only or mostly typographic Stories = good for `20-30%` of story output
- image, video, lifestyle, repost, or native-interaction-led Stories = should make up the majority

Why:

- text-only Stories are strong for brand consistency
- image-led or interaction-led Stories are stronger for attention and action

## When To Use Images

Use images when:

- the goal is empathy
- the goal is realism
- the goal is routine context
- the goal is proof
- the story needs to feel more human than editorial

Do not force images when:

- the message works better as a quiet text-led bridge
- the frame is purely educational reinforcement
- an image would only add noise

## Story Design Rules

Stories still need to feel like Mitozz.

Keep:

- premium whitespace
- stable typography
- dark charcoal text
- calm mist-blue and cloud-white atmosphere
- soft, restrained support visuals

Avoid:

- app-like fake buttons
- cluttered stickers everywhere
- trendy chaos
- random color drift
- visual tone that feels cheaper than the feed

## Story Interaction Rules

Use native story tools intentionally.

Approved interaction tools:

- poll sticker
- emoji slider
- question box
- link sticker

Best practice:

- one primary interaction per frame
- keep enough safe space so the sticker does not fight the design
- do not build fake sticker graphics into the artwork

## Story CTA Rules

Feed reinforcement Stories:

- route back to the feed post
- use elegant text plus native sticker if needed

Conversion-support Stories:

- route to the most relevant landing page

Rule:

- artwork guides
- native sticker clicks

Do not rely on fake buttons inside the design.

## Recommended Story Sequence Formula

For most 3-frame story sets:

1. Hook
2. Context or interaction
3. CTA

Best Mitozz version:

1. brand-clean opening frame
2. human, contextual, or interactive middle frame
3. elegant CTA frame with space for sticker or directional cue

## Highlight Structure

Keep Highlights simple and strategic.

Recommended highlight buckets:

- `About`
- `Mitochondria`
- `Routine`
- `Trust`
- `FAQ`
- `Reviews`

Rule:

- Stories disappear
- Highlights preserve the best recurring education and trust assets

## Operating Rule

Do not ask:

- "does every Story need to be beautiful?"

Ask:

- "does this Story help the brand feel present, useful, and easy to act on?"

For Mitozz, the best strategy is:

- keep the feed more polished
- keep Stories slightly lighter and more alive
- keep both inside the same premium system
